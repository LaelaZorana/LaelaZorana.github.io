About

Ten years of building and quality work, now pointed at AI.

My career has moved through systems where mistakes are expensive, in banking, risk, and large operations. That taught me to prove things work before they ship, not after.

Now I do that for machine learning. On the build side I train neural networks: a multilayer perceptron coded from scratch in NumPy, a convolutional network fine tuned for vision, a transformer fine tuned for language, and an LSTM for time series. On the quality side I build the tools that catch bad models and bad data before they cost anyone: rubric scoring, RLHF preference rating, inter rater agreement, and package validation.

Every project below ships two things together: the model and the evidence it works. Each card has a live demo you can open and click in under a minute.

Neural Net From Scratch

A multilayer perceptron written by hand in NumPy, no framework. Forward pass, backprop, softmax, and Adam all coded from zero. Its gradients are checked against finite differences in the test suite, so the math is proven, not assumed.

CNN + Grad-CAM

A ResNet18 fine tuned by transfer learning from ImageNet to a new task, ants versus bees. A hand written Grad-CAM draws a heatmap of where the network actually looked, so you can check it keyed on the insect and not the background.

AI Agent Scenario QC

Paste an agent training scenario and get back a weighted rubric score out of 100 and a severity tagged defect log: undeclared tools, undefined personas, out of order steps, empty success criteria. It catches the quiet defects before a labeling team ever sees them.
rubric + defects10 tests

RLHF Pairwise Rater

The workflow behind preference data for RLHF. Rate two model responses on four axes, and the tool flags self consistency errors and measures inter rater agreement with Cohen's kappa, so you can tell whether two people apply the same standard.
Cohen's kappaconsistency check

SCORM QA Validator

E-learning packages fail silently on upload, a missing launch file just shows a blank screen. This tool opens the package, parses the manifest, and hands you a defect log with severity and location before it ever ships to an LMS.
